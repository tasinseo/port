Types of Monitor available in BD

Monitors can be classified by type based on some features and mechanisms. There are different types of Monitors available in the market. The most common monitors are:

LCD Monitors & LED Monitors

Liquid crystal displays, or LCDs, are what the acronym stands for.LCD monitors can have excellent resolutions and excellent color and image quality. However, they have a slow refresh rate and perhaps a slow response time.

An LED stands for a light-emitting diode. The color and image quality of LED displays, also known as LED-backlit monitors, are excellent, but their response times are poor. They are therefore suitable for activities like gaming or rendering that need precise motions.

Gaming Monitor

The gaming monitor usually has a higher refresh rate which is one of the most differences between the other monitors. The purpose of gaming monitors is to maximize the visual quality of the graphics card and CPU output. They are in charge of showing the end result of all of your computer's picture rendering and processing, yet their depiction of color, motion, and image sharpness.

Curved Monitor

The most recent in monitor technology, curved monitors can be pricey. Curved monitors are becoming more and more common among gamers, Video Editors, Content Creators, and programmers. Curved monitors are now used by the majority of professionals in the workforce.

Touch Monitor

Touching a touch screen display allows you to interact with it. This function allows users to interact with the computer using a finger or stylus instead of using a mouse or keyboard. Compared to standard computer monitors, touch screens can provide more functionality.

Ultra-wide Monitor

A standard 34-inch ultrawide display has a 3,440 x 1,440 resolution. Contrast this with a normal 2,560 x 1,440-pixel 27-inch business display. You gain 880 pixels horizontally, for a total increase of nearly 1.3 million pixels. Additionally, it's ideal for using two or more windows simultaneously.

Buying Guide for Computer Monitor

If you are planning to purchase a monitor for your daily use or office work or gaming, you can consider some key features of a modern monitor. Gamers should emphasize rapid refresh rates and quick response times, professionals should focus on color fidelity, and regular users have less precise criteria yet frequently select for a VA panel. However, you should also consider the following factors when choosing a monitor.

Monitor Size

Small monitors, 22 inches or less, are used for basic computing when money and space are tight. 23- to 24-inch monitors are a good size for most workstations and needs. Also small enough to double with a two-screen workstation for affordable multi-screen flexibility.Many professionals choose 25- to 29-inch 4K displays for superior clarity and color representation. Larger screens have additional capabilities that help people who stare at the monitor for lengthy durations (see below for a discussion of these features).Widescreen monitors over 40" have a different aspect ratio than regular displays. The selection of outstanding 30-inch and larger monitors grows. One ultra-widescreen monitor can replace two smaller ones without interrupting the display.

Refresh Rate

A display's refresh rate describes how many times it can draw a new image per second. It is measured in hertz (Hz). For example, if your display has a 144Hz refresh rate, the image refreshes 144 times per second. Larger values ​​mean better, smoother images. Refresh rate is especially important for gamers, who will want a monitor with at least 75Hz (most monitors are designed to offer at least 120Hz gaming), combined with the lowest response time you can find. If you're not gaming, a 60Hz refresh rate should work. if you want, you can choose a monitor with a maximum refresh rate of 360.

Response time

Shorter response times are preferable, but not crucial unless you're gaming. Response time measures how long it takes a display to convert pixels from black to white or from one shade of gray to another. Longer response times can cause motion blur in games and films. The quickest gaming displays have a response time of 0.5ms.

Connectors & Ports

HDMI is the industry standard connector for consumer-oriented computer monitors, making it simple to connect a monitor to a laptop, though some laptops, particularly thin and light models, may require a dongle.DisplayPort was an attempt to replace the VGA and DVI interfaces.DisplayPort is supported by some of the best top-tier gaming PC monitors USB Type-C ports are the most popular port it's mostly used for pc and smartphones.

Other Features

Built-in speakers, headphone jacks, RGB lighting, and USB connections for attaching peripherals like Mouse and keyboards are extra features some monitors may include. Depending on your using purpose, these could or might not be useful.
